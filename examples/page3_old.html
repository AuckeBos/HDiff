    Titel: Build cross-platform desktop apps with JavaScript

  2. dinsdag 4 oktober, 12.30-13.30, HG00.307 (georganiseerd door Thalia, hier aanmelden als je Thalia-lid bent)

    Spreker: Pepijn Noltes van Thales

    Titel: Software development for modern radar systems

    Abstract:

    The software department of the Application Engineering of Thales Netherlands is responsible for the software development in radar- and optical sensors for worldwide navies- and air defense systems. The work is performed in multi-disciplinary teams with specialists in system engineering, functional sensor design and design and realization of real-time embedded systems.

    Pepijn Noltes is a Software Architect at Thales and part of a team responsible for the overall software architecture of Radar systems. He is also a member of the Apache Software Foundation and active in several studies concerning reconfigurable and robust software architectures.

    In this presentation Pepijn will talk about the software development challenges involved in creating innovative Radar systems and how this is tackled at Thales. A few of the challenges presented will be:

    How to design and develop high performance, time critical and distributed software solutions within a limited budget on time? What software technologies and processes are used to create state of art Radar systems with the capability of detecting targets at 2000 km? How does Thales ensures that these systems will operate for at least 30 years and how does this influence the technology choices? How to maximize the benefits of an abundance of software technologies (for illustration there are currently more than 35 million GitHub repositories) without locking in specific technologies or trends? Can you combine military projects with open source development? How does our future architecture looks like (and what does it solve)?

  6. dinsdag 20 december, 12.30-13.30, HG00.307 (buiten Thalia om)

    Spreker: Max Hinne

    Titel: From the "global brain" to regular brains

    Abstract:

    Few areas in science are so interdisciplinary as the study of networks. To name a few application examples: social network analysis, protein interactions, ecosystems, the WWW and the internet, traffic analysis, drug studies, neuroscience and graph theory. That progress in so many different areas can be made, using similar tools, is remarkable.

    In this talk I discuss how I became (and still am) enamoured by the study of networks. Along the way I'll show some examples of my research, starting out with simple clustering problems on Web graphs, and moving on to more complex machine learning issues on large-scale networks within the human brain. Throughout, I'll talk about the scientific process and how it can be rewarding and challenging at the same time.

  12. dinsdag 2 mei, 12.30-13.30, HG00.307 (buiten Thalia om)

    Spreker: Judith van Stegeren

    Titel: Breaking the bank. Tracing defacers through a company network.

    [ slides ]

    Abstract:

    In this talk, I will talk about my work as an incident responder at the National Cyber Security Centre (NCSC-NL), which is the Dutch Computer Emergency Response Team (CERT) for the Dutch government and vital infrastructure. I will take you through an example security incident, which focuses on a defacement attack on a fictional bank. On the basis of this scenario, I will demonstrate how you can track an attacker through your network by using network forensics tools and security logs. Do not attend this talk if you're afraid of network protocols or linux command line tools. ;)
